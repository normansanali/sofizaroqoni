Helen Journalist

After completing her education, Helen was accepted on the Daily Mail’s program for trainee sub-editors, working in the job for a few years, and later joining the team responsible for commissioning features for the newspaper. She was appointed in the Humanities Honorary Writing Fellow at Oxford University for 2018/2019 and is now on the steering committee for the Reuters Institute for Journalism at Oxford University

For five years from August 2006, she ran a networking event, open to all young journalists, known as  Schmooze and Booze, for which she arranged for events held in a central London pub every other month. Helen commented in 2007 that older colleagues, who had worked with each other for quite a long time, all seemed to know each other, while her colleagues did not.

In May 2012 Helen was appointed as deputy editor of the New Statesman after becoming assistant editor in 2010. In 2012, Lewis coined the light-hearted Lewis’s Law known as the comments on any article about feminism justify feminism, She has written about the harassment of women online and trolling. She has been a staff writer at The Atlantic since July 2019 and in November 2019, April 2020, October 2020, and April 2021  Helen was a panelist on BBC’s Have I Got News for You.

Helen Book

Helen has published a book known as, Difficult Women: A History of Feminism in 11 Fights. The book was published on 27 February 2020. In her book, Helen tries to give us an unconventional history of feminism.
