Why You Sometimes See The Moon During The Day

published on July 19, 2024
According to Live Science, if the Earth didn't have an atmosphere, we would actually be able to see the moon all month long, because it is out during the day every day except during its full moon phase. The Earth's atmosphere scatters blue light from the sun across the sky, giving it a light blue shade.
