Who was the 2013 FSU football coach?

The 2013 Florida State University (FSU) football coach was Jimbo Fisher. Fisher took over as head coach in 2010 and had great success during his tenure with the Seminoles. In 2013, Fisher led FSU to an outstanding season, which culminated in a national championship victory. Let's take a closer look at Jimbo Fisher and the remarkable achievements of the 2013 FSU football team.

Jimbo Fisher: The Head Coach

Jimbo Fisher was the head coach of the Florida State Seminoles from 2010 to 2017. He took over the reins from legendary coach Bobby Bowden and continued the tradition of excellence at FSU. Fisher's fourth year as head coach was in 2013, and it turned out to be a memorable one for the Seminoles.

The 2013 FSU Football Season

Under Jimbo Fisher's guidance, the 2013 FSU football team had an exceptional season. They finished the regular season with an undefeated 13-0 record, winning the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) championship along the way. The team's success earned them a spot in the prestigious BCS National Championship Game.

Road to the National Championship

The Seminoles faced tough competition throughout the season but dominated their opponents. Led by quarterback Jameis Winston, who won the Heisman Trophy that year, FSU boasted a high-powered offense that scored an average of 51.6 points per game.

In the ACC Championship Game, FSU faced the Duke Blue Devils and emerged victorious with a resounding 45-7 win. This win secured their place in the BCS National Championship Game against the Auburn Tigers.

The BCS National Championship Game

The 2014 BCS National Championship Game took place on January 6, 2014, at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. The Seminoles faced a formidable opponent in the Auburn Tigers, who were also undefeated that season.

In a thrilling back-and-forth game, the Seminoles found themselves trailing in the fourth quarter. However, with just 13 seconds remaining, Jameis Winston threw a touchdown pass to Kelvin Benjamin, securing a 34-31 victory for FSU and their third national championship in school history.

Legacy and Impact

The 2013 FSU football team's national championship victory under Jimbo Fisher's leadership left a lasting impact on the program. It marked a return to dominance for the Seminoles and solidified their status as a powerhouse in college football.

Fisher's success as head coach laid the foundation for future accomplishments by the Florida State football program. His tenure at FSU included multiple conference championships and consistently high rankings in the national polls.

In 2013, Jimbo Fisher served as the head coach of the Florida State Seminoles football team. Under his leadership, FSU had an undefeated season and won the ACC Championship. The Seminoles went on to claim the BCS National Championship with a thrilling victory over the Auburn Tigers. Fisher's tenure at FSU was marked by success and helped establish the program as a force to be reckoned with in college football.
