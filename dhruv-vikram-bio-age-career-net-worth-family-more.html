Dhruv Vikram career

Dhruv was initially requested to appear in Mohan Raja’s 2004 film M. Kumaran Son of Mahalakshmi, but he ultimately declined.

The character of Anbukkarasu in Pasanga (2008) was later offered to him by Pandiraj, but Vikram was reluctant to let his son pursue acting, therefore Kishore DS was cast in the part instead.

In 2016, Dhruv released a short film called “Good Night Charlie” on YouTube that was centered on child abuse.

Bala’s Varmaa, a remake of the Telugu film Arjun Reddy (2017), was to have been Dhruv’s feature film debut; however, the release of the film was delayed since the producer was dissatisfied with the finished product.

The movie was relaunched as Adithya Varma, directed by Gireesaaya, with Dhruv continuing his role, and released in 2019.

M. Suganth of The Times of India noted that Dhruv Vikram gives a performance that captures Vijay Deverakonda’s intense and raw act from the original note for note, but there is an assurance and honesty here that makes us appreciate it rather than just dismiss it as an imitation.

Suganth said of Dhruv Vikram that “he is rawer here, looking sure-footed in some scenes and like a novice in others,” when Varmaa was finally released in 2020.

In his father’s 60th movie, Mahaan, which debuted on 10 February 2022 on Amazon Prime Video, Dhruv appeared with his father.

He also co-wrote and performed the song “Missing Me” for the movie. Later, Dhruv penned, sung, and directed the music video for the song “Manase” by Ujwal Gupta.

Dhruv has commitments for two untitled films, one to be directed by Ganesh K. Babu and the other by Mari Selvaraj, both of which are based on the life of kabaddi player Manathi Ganesan.

He was performing the solo songs “Oru Kaayam” by Ujwal Gupta and “Poomadhiye” by Santhosh Narayanan in the middle of 2023.
